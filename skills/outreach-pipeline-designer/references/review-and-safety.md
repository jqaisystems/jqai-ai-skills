# Review And Safety

Use this reference before recommending exports, sending, scraping, enrichment, or CRM updates.

## Required Human Approvals

Require human approval before:

- Sending email, DM, forms, comments, or social messages.
- Exporting lead lists or contact data.
- Scheduling follow-ups.
- Updating a CRM or system of record.
- Enriching contact-level data from paid or sensitive sources.
- Publishing screenshots, logs, dashboards, or example records.

## Source Boundaries

Prefer:

- User-provided data the user is allowed to use.
- Public business websites and public company pages.
- Manual research notes.
- Fake sample data for docs, examples, demos, and GitHub.

Avoid:

- Private contact data without permission.
- Scraping behind logins or paywalls.
- Instructions to bypass platform limits.
- Sensitive personal data, protected categories, or medical/financial/legal targeting.
- Publishing raw exports or prospect lists.

## Outreach Draft Rules

- Be specific, concise, and respectful.
- Ground personalization in public business context.
- Do not invent facts.
- Do not imply a relationship that does not exist.
- Include a clear next step.
- Preserve opt-out and suppression logic.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and ask for human review when:

- Data provenance is unclear.
- The source might violate platform terms.
- A draft uses sensitive personal assumptions.
- A lead is in a suppressed, rejected, or opted-out state.
- The workflow would send, publish, spend, or update records without approval.

## Public Sharing Boundary

Public artifacts may show the workflow, fake records, schema, review states, and aggregate outcomes. Keep out real prospects, emails, phone numbers, company lists, private scoring prompts, logs, database files, API keys, and live account configuration.
