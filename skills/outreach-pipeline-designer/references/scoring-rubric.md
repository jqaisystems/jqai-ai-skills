# Scoring Rubric

Use a simple, transparent score so humans can understand why a lead is prioritized.

## Default Score Bands

| Score | Priority | Meaning | Default Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80-100 | Hot | Strong fit and clear evidence of need | Human reviews draft and evidence |
| 60-79 | Warm | Plausible fit with some evidence | Human reviews before any outreach |
| 40-59 | Research | Weak or incomplete evidence | Gather more context or defer |
| 0-39 | Skip | Poor fit, missing relevance, or suppression signal | Reject or suppress |

## Suggested Criteria

Score only from evidence the user is allowed to use:

- Fit: the account matches the target segment.
- Need signal: public evidence suggests the offer could be relevant.
- Timing: there is a current event, launch, hiring signal, new page, or other timing clue.
- Value potential: the account appears able to benefit from the offer.
- Personalization quality: there is enough context to write a specific, respectful message.
- Risk: low risk of wrong-person targeting, sensitive category issues, or compliance concerns.

## Score Reason Format

Use short, inspectable explanations:

```text
Score: 72
Priority: Warm
Reason: Strong segment fit and clear public website gap, but timing signal is weak and contact route needs human confirmation.
Recommended next action: Add to review queue; do not send until owner approves message and contact route.
```

## Avoid

- Black-box scores without reasons.
- Scores based on private, sensitive, or unverified personal data.
- Draft generation for records with low confidence.
- Treating AI scores as permission to send.
