# Keyword Workflow

Use this workflow when the user provides current tags, keyword-tool exports, competitor notes, or a plain product description.

## Step 1: Understand Intent

Identify:

- Product type.
- Buyer use case.
- Style or aesthetic.
- Material or format.
- Audience or recipient.
- Occasion or season.
- Any accuracy limits.

## Step 2: Classify Current Keywords

Use these labels:

- `keep`: accurate and useful.
- `merge`: overlaps with another tag or title phrase.
- `replace`: weak, irrelevant, misleading, too broad, or risky.
- `test`: plausible but needs data or human judgment.

## Step 3: Choose Replacements

Prefer replacements that:

- Match the actual product.
- Represent clear buyer intent.
- Add coverage not already present.
- Support a natural title or description.
- Avoid trademark, competitor, or sensitive claims.

## Step 4: Explain Rejections

For rejected keywords, give a short reason:

```text
Rejected: "luxury wall art"
Reason: Too broad and not specific enough to the product format.
```

## Step 5: Output

Return:

- Current keyword audit.
- Recommended title phrase.
- Final tag set.
- Missing keyword opportunities.
- Rejected keywords and reasons.
- Suggested next test.

## Public Examples

Use fake products and fake metrics in public examples. Do not include raw keyword-tool exports, shop names, listing IDs, or private performance data unless the user explicitly approves them for public use.
